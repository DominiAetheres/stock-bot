Getting Started

To get started with the StockBot application, you first need to create an account. You can do this by clicking on the "Register" link in the navigation bar and filling out the registration form. Once you've created an account, you can log in and start using the StockBot.

Using the StockBot

The StockBot is designed to simulate conversation with human users. To use the StockBot, simply click on the "Home" link in the navigation bar. You will be taken to a page where you can type your questions and receive answers in a conversational manner.

To get information on a particular share, type the keyword "info" followed by a colon and the name of the share. For example, to get information on Apple Inc., type "info:aapl". The available keywords for information include "dividendyield", "dividendpershare", "eps", "peratio", and "profitmargin". Simply replace "info" with any of these keywords to get the corresponding information on the share you are interested in.

Alternatively to get a general overview of some stocks, the keyword "overview" could be used to return a description of the stock, and some of its important statistics such as its current price or price-earnings ratio. It could be used to compare companies in a similar industry like "overview:ibm,msft,aapl".

To get the price of a share, type the keyword "price" followed by a comma and a time frame. The time frame can be either a word from "current, yesterday", or "last week, last month, last year". For example, to get the current price of Apple Inc.'s share, type "price,current:aapl". This will give you the current price of the share. Similarly, to get the price of the share from last week, type "price,lastweek:aapl".

Viewing Your Conversation History

If you're logged in, you can view your StockBot conversation history by clicking on the "History" link in the navigation bar. This will take you to a page where you can see all your previous conversations with the StockBot. You can also perform a search within your StockBot history to retrieve specific information or conversations.

Logging Out

To log out of the StockBot application, simply click on the "Logout" link in the navigation bar.
